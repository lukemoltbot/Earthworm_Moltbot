## Key Geophysical Response Characteristics

### 1. **Density Log Responses**
- **Coal**: Very low density (1.2-1.8 g/cc) due to high organic content and porosity
- **Shales**: Moderate density (2.4-2.8 g/cc) increasing with compaction
- **Sandstones**: Variable density (2.2-2.65 g/cc) depending on porosity and mineralogy
- **Carbonates**: High density (2.71-2.9+ g/cc) with limestone typically 2.71 g/cc

### 2. **Gamma Ray Log Responses**
- **Clean formations**: Low API values (5-60 API)
- **Shaley formations**: High API values (80-140+ API)
- **Coal**: Typically low (10-30 API) but can vary with ash content
- **Carbonates**: Very low (5-20 API) when pure

### 3. **Resistivity Log Responses**
- **Coal**: Very high resistivity (100-2000+ ohm-m) due to insulating properties
- **Water-bearing formations**: Low to moderate resistivity (5-100 ohm-m)
- **Hydrocarbon-bearing formations**: High resistivity (100-1000+ ohm-m)
- **Shales**: Low resistivity (5-20 ohm-m) due to conductive clay minerals

## CoalLog v3.1 Specific Notes

Based on research into Australian coal geology standards:

1. **CoalLog v3.1** is the Australian standard for coal geophysical logging
2. It includes specific lithology codes for coal-bearing sequences
3. The standard emphasizes accurate identification of:
   - Coal seams and their quality
   - Roof and floor strata
   - Partings within coal seams
   - Geological structures affecting mining
