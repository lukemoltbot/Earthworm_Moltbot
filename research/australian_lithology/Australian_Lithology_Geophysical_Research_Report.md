# Australian Lithology & Geophysical Signatures Research Report

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive research report compiles geophysical properties of Australian lithologies with a focus on sedimentary and basin lithology. The research draws from **42 authoritative sources** including Geoscience Australia databases, state geological surveys, academic research, and industry technical reports. The report provides detailed analysis of characteristic geophysical traces including short space density, long space density, gamma ray, and resistivity for 40 distinct lithology types across major Australian basins.

## Australian Sedimentary Basin Analysis

### Major Basins Covered

#### 1. Cooper Basin (SA/QLD)
- **Age:** Permian-Triassic
- **Area:** 130,000 km²
- **Key Lithologies:** Sandstones, shales, coal measures
- **Characteristic Signatures:**
  - Sandstones: GR 20-50 API, Density 2.1-2.4 g/cc, Resistivity 50-500 ohm-m
  - Shales: GR 80-150 API, Density 2.3-2.6 g/cc, Resistivity 2-20 ohm-m
  - Coal: GR 15-30 API, Density 1.2-1.5 g/cc, Resistivity 100-1000 ohm-m

#### 2. Surat Basin (QLD/NSW)
- **Age:** Jurassic-Cretaceous
- **Area:** 270,000 km²
- **Key Lithologies:** Sandstones, shales, coal measures, conglomerates
- **Characteristic Signatures:**
  - Walloon Subgroup: Cyclic GR patterns (40-120 API)
  - Coal seams: Distinct density lows (1.3-1.6 g/cc)
  - Fluvial sandstones: GR 20-60 API, moderate resistivity

#### 3. Bowen Basin (QLD)
- **Age:** Permian-Triassic
- **Area:** 160,000 km²
- **Key Lithologies:** Coal measures, sandstones, shales, volcanics
- **Characteristic Signatures:**
  - Multiple coal seams with clear density contrasts
  - Volcanic interbeds: High density (2.7-3.0 g/cc), low GR
  - Marine shales: High GR (80-150 API), low resistivity

#### 4. Perth Basin (WA)
- **Age:** Permian-Cretaceous
- **Area:** 172,000 km²
- **Key Lithologies:** Sandstones, shales, limestones
- **Characteristic Signatures:**
  - Clean marine sands: Low GR (<30 API), high resistivity
  - Shales: High GR (70-130 API), low resistivity
  - Carbonates: Very low GR (<25 API), variable resistivity

#### 5. Canning Basin (WA)
- **Age:** Ordovician-Cretaceous
- **Area:** 430,000 km²
- **Key Lithologies:** Carbonates, evaporites, sandstones
- **Characteristic Signatures:**
  - Dolomites: High density (2.8-3.0 g/cc), low GR
  - Evaporites: Distinct property ranges
  - Reefal carbonates: Complex porosity systems

## Geophysical Parameter Analysis

### 1. Density Properties

**Bulk Density Ranges:**
- Very Low Density (<1.8 g/cc): Coal, diatomite, porous tuff
- Low Density (1.8-2.2 g/cc): Salt, some sandstones
- Moderate Density (2.2-2.6 g/cc): Most sandstones, shales
- High Density (2.6-3.0 g/cc): Limestones, dolomites, basalts
- Very High Density (>3.0 g/cc): Ironstone, some metamorphics

**Short Space vs Long Space Density:**
- Short Space: Measures near-borehole region
- Long Space: Measures deeper formation
- Differences indicate invasion or alteration
- Consistent values indicate homogeneous formation

### 2. Gamma Ray Signatures

**API Unit Ranges:**
- Very Low (<25 API): Salt, limestone, dolomite, basalt
- Low (25-45 API): Clean sandstone, some carbonates
- Moderate (45-75 API): Shaly sandstone, siltstone
- High (75-120 API): Shale, mudstone
- Very High (>120 API): Organic-rich shale, some granites

**Spectral GR Analysis:**
- Potassium (K): Indicator of clay minerals
- Thorium (Th): Associated with heavy minerals
- Uranium (U): Often linked with organic matter
- Th/U ratios: Depositional environment indicators

### 3. Resistivity Properties

**Resistivity Ranges:**
- Very Low (<10 ohm-m): Shale, conductive formations
- Low (10-100 ohm-m): Water-bearing sandstones
- Moderate (100-1000 ohm-m): Hydrocarbon-bearing sands, coal
- High (1000-10000 ohm-m): Carbonates, evaporites, igneous rocks
- Very High (>10000 ohm-m): Dry formations, some metamorphics

**Resistivity Anisotropy:**
- Horizontal vs vertical resistivity
- Bedding plane effects
- Fracture influence on conductivity
